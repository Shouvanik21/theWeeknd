THE WEEKND

Am I the shadow or the man ?

For two years, I’ve walked these streets as Batman. I’ve become a creature of the night, striking terror into criminals. I don’t work in the light—I work in the shadows. They don’t see me coming, but they know I’m there. Every scream, every whispered rumor, every glance into the darkness—it’s me. Gotham is chaos, but I’ve learned how to use it. My mind is my greatest weapon, but my fists speak the language they understand. This city bleeds, and I am its reckoning.

I am vengeance and I am the shadows. I was born the night my parents were killed—gunned down in front of me in a cold Gotham alley. Their deaths shattered my world, leaving me with nothing but anger and fear. That night, something inside me broke, but something else was forged: a mission. Gotham is a city drowning in corruption, where crime thrives in every corner. I made a vow to bring justice, to fight the evil that took them. But justice isn’t enough. They need to fear me.

I don’t do this for recognition. I don’t care if they call me a hero. I wear the mask because Bruce Wayne died with his parents. Batman is who I’ve become—a tool of fear, vengeance, and something deeper. This city is broken, but I won’t stop. I will fight for Gotham, even if it consumes me. Because this is my purpose. This is who I am.
